Swedish Structural Timber C24

We import certified C24 grade timber from Sweden — kiln-dried, straight and ready to build with. It comes from Södra, a Swedish forestry cooperative representing over 50,000 forest owners.

C24 timber meets EN 338 and has a characteristic bending strength of 24 MPa. A higher standard than domestic timber — required by most structural projects.

Ideal applications: roof trusses, floor joists, load-bearing walls and structural frames. Every piece marked with green Södra tags and red C24 grade stamps.

  • C24 certified (EN 338)
  • Kiln-dried
  • Södra origin — Sweden
  • Structural applications

What Does C24 Grade Mean?

C24 is a European structural strength grade for softwood timber, defined by EN 338. The letter C stands for conifer, and 24 indicates a characteristic bending strength of 24 MPa.

The classification is based on timber strength, stiffness and density. C24 timber is graded visually and mechanically, ensuring consistent quality. It is used wherever calculated structural load-bearing capacity is required.

Typical applications: roof trusses, floor beams, load-bearing walls, columns and frame elements. Designers and building inspectors increasingly require documented timber grade.

Quality Badges

Every piece of Södra timber bears two markings: a green Södra brand tag (proof of Swedish origin and kiln-drying) and a red C24 grade stamp (structural strength certificate to EN 338).
